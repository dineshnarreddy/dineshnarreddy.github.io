What the assistant can help with

Irrigation

Tells you when to water, how much, and for how long – based on soil moisture, crop stage, weather forecast and pump capacity.

Fertilizer

Stage-wise nutrition plans that respect local costs and avoid over-use of chemicals, with templates for each growth phase.

Pest & Disease

Weather-based outbreak alerts plus image-based diagnosis from leaf photos and drone images – more than just a leaf-scanner app.

Why this matters

  • Reduce guesswork – decisions based on data, not only tradition.
  • Save water, energy and fertilizer – fewer unnecessary irrigations and sprays.
  • Protect yield – early warnings for high disease-risk days.
  • Works offline or with weak internet – runs on a small edge device on the farm.

The goal is simple: practical AI that small and medium farmers can trust, not a black box. Every recommendation comes with a short “why” explanation.

Two modes: open-field and greenhouse

Open-field Mango & Other Crops

Designed first for mango orchards in places like Andhra Pradesh, and extendable to other crops.

  • Uses soil sensors where available, plus simple local weather data.
  • Works with WhatsApp images and basic phones.
  • Typical message: “Irrigate Plot 2 for 30 minutes this evening. Rain risk tomorrow is low.”
Learn more about open-field mode →

Greenhouse (Tomato, later other crops)

For climate-controlled greenhouses that want to cut energy and CO₂ while keeping plants comfortable.

  • Uses internal climate sensors + outside weather and energy data.
  • Suggests smart temperature, ventilation, screen and irrigation settings.
  • Built from real tomato greenhouse data and able to show estimated energy savings.

Backed by research, built with farmers in mind

This assistant is part of an ongoing PhD project on AI & IoT for smallholder farmers at a UK university. It combines a systematic literature review, simulation studies and real farm & greenhouse trials in India and the UK.

The vision is to keep the core technology affordable and accessible for small farmers, while also offering advanced features for commercial greenhouses.
