How it works

A simple pipeline ‚Äì collect data, run AI on the edge, send clear advice back to the farmer or greenhouse manager.

  1. 1. Collect data

    Sensors (soil moisture, temperature, humidity), weather (IMD, NASA POWER), soil type, crop variety and simple images from phones or drones.

  2. 2. Analyse on the edge device

    A Raspberry Pi or similar device runs lightweight ML models locally, so the system keeps working even with poor connectivity.

  3. 3. AI decision engine

    Irrigation, fertilizer and pest risk modules combine rules with ML to generate recommendations tailored to each field or greenhouse.

  4. 4. Explainable recommendations

    Every suggestion comes with a short explanation like: ‚ÄúSoil is dry, temperature is high and no rain is expected.‚Äù

  5. 5. Farmer interface

    Advice appears as WhatsApp messages, app notifications or a greenhouse dashboard that staff can act on immediately.

Backed by research, built with farmers in mind

MangoEdgeAI, GreenhouseEdgeAI and the future MultiCropEdgeAI are part of an ongoing PhD project on AI & IoT for smallholder farmers.

Academic foundation

Developed as PhD research at a UK university, combining a systematic literature review, simulations and real-world pilots in India and the UK.

Tech stack

Edge-first architecture using Raspberry Pi, LoRa / MQTT, TimescaleDB, classical ML and neural networks exported to TensorFlow Lite.

Farmer-first principles

Focus on explainable AI, low running costs and designs that can work with basic devices and limited connectivity.
