About the project

Mango Edge AI is a PhD research project focused on affordable, reliable farm decisions for small-scale mango growers in Kadiri, Andhra Pradesh (India). The system combines low-power IoT sensors (soil moisture, humidity, temperature) with machine learning models to give actionable advice farmers can trust.

  • Why now?

    Water stress, rising input costs, and climate variability make timely decisions critical. Our offline-first approach works even with limited connectivity.

  • Who benefits?

    Smallholder mango farmers, local extension workers, and CSR/impact partners supporting sustainable agriculture in rural communities.

Features

Current

  • Edge device (Raspberry Pi) with MQTT + TimescaleDB
  • Irrigation recommendation — explainable rules + ML baseline
  • Simple dashboard & SMS/WhatsApp alerts

Next 6–12 months

  • Fertilizer planning (rules → ML)
  • Pest risk signals (TFLite on device)
  • Weather fusion (IMD, Open‑Meteo, NASA POWER)

Future

  • Farmer mobile app (offline-first)
  • Model fine‑tuning on local farm data
  • Scaling to tomatoes (UK comparison) & other crops

Research & Collaboration

This project is conducted as part of a PhD at the University of Portsmouth. We welcome collaboration with researchers, NGOs, and CSR teams interested in evaluating field impact and co-developing deployments.
